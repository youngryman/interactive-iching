The Interactive I Ching

No yarrow sticks, no coins, no arithmetic. Just you, the uncontrollable yin-yang lines… and pure chance.

The I Ching, or Book of Changes, is an ancient Chinese system holding that whatever happens arises from the interplay of two forces, yin and yang. To consult it, a pattern of six yin-and-yang lines is formed by chance and read for its meaning.

Older methods generated numbers and assigned them to lines. This one is immediate — you pull a line directly from a display of every possible line, moving faster than you can react, so the result is pure chance.

— Daniel Young
